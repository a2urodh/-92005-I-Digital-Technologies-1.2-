Our Courses

Computer Science Courses at Saint Kentigern College

At Saint Kentigern College, our Computer Science courses provide students with a robust foundation in technology and innovation. Whether you're pursuing the IB, NCEA, or looking ahead to tertiary education, our courses are designed to spark creativity and prepare you for the future.

International Baccalaureate (IB) Computer Science

The IB Computer Science course offers a rigorous, globally recognized curriculum that combines theoretical knowledge with practical application. Students delve into core topics such as algorithms, data structures, and programming, and engage in a substantial project that addresses real-world problems. This course enhances critical thinking and problem-solving skills, preparing students for prestigious universities worldwide.

NCEA Computer Science

The NCEA Computer Science course focuses on practical and theoretical aspects tailored to New Zealand's education system. Students complete hands-on projects and practical assignments, covering programming, digital technologies, and systems development. This course provides relevant skills for local and international study and employment opportunities, with a strong emphasis on real-world applications.

Tertiary Education in Computer Science

Tertiary education in Computer Science includes undergraduate and postgraduate programs that offer advanced study and specialization. Students engage in in-depth coursework and research, gaining expertise in areas such as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and software engineering. These programs provide a pathway to advanced careers and research opportunities, with valuable industry connections and practical experience.
